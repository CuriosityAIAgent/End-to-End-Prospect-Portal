Prospecting Brief: A Systematic Approach
Purpose. Prospecting fails when it's opportunistic and ad hoc. A systematic approach treats the existing client base and the wider JPMorgan network as a structured asset to be mapped, mined, and worked deliberately — rather than waiting for introductions to arrive. The goal is a repeatable process that produces a steady flow of qualified, warm-routed introductions to UHNW prospects in our coverage area (PE partners, hedge fund principals, and their orbit).
The three channels
1. Referrals from existing clients. The highest-conversion channel. Clients introduce people like themselves — which, in our segment, means other partners, co-investors, and fund principals. These prospects arrive pre-qualified on wealth, pre-warmed on trust, and aligned on profile. The constraint is rarely willingness; it's that we ask infrequently, vaguely, or at the wrong moment.
2. JPMorgan network referral. Internal cross-LOB flow — Commercial/Investment Bank coverage teams, the markets desk, asset management, and colleagues across the franchise sit on relationships with exactly the founders, GPs and principals we want. A liquidity event the IB advised on is a private-banking opportunity. The constraint here is visibility and reciprocity: knowing who internally holds the relationship, and being the banker they think of.
3. Cold approach. Lowest yield, but necessary to fill gaps the warm channels don't reach. Most effective when it isn't truly cold — i.e. anchored to a shared affiliation (fund, deal, alma mater, board, charity, club) so there's a credible reason for the outreach. Pure cold contact should be the exception, not the engine.
Client profile — the dimensions to map
For every existing client, and every prospect, build out the same profile. This is what turns a name into a route.
	• Employer and previous employer — the fund, the prior shop, the spin-outs. Partners cluster; ex-colleagues from a previous firm are often the richest seam.
	• Personal profile — career history, liquidity events, age/career stage, where they sit in the wealth lifecycle.
	• Family connections — spouse, siblings, the next generation, family-office structures. Wealth is rarely held by one individual in isolation.
	• Hobbies and interests — clubs, sports, collecting, anything that creates a non-work setting for connection (relevant given racket sports, etc., as genuine common ground).
	• Charity work — boards, trustee roles, galas, foundations. Philanthropy puts UHNW individuals in the same rooms and is a natural, low-pressure introduction context.
The point of profiling is not the data itself but the overlaps: where two clients share a former employer, a co-investment, a school, a charity board — each overlap is a potential referral path.
The four operational questions
Where are the touchpoints with current clients? Map the natural, recurring moments where a referral conversation is appropriate and welcome: portfolio reviews, post-transaction debriefs, year-end planning, life events (liquidity, exit, succession), and social/hospitality settings. Don't ask for referrals cold mid-relationship; tie the ask to a moment where the client is already feeling well-served.
Where in the network can we find a referral? Run two passes. Internally: which JPMorgan colleagues cover the funds, founders, or counterparties adjacent to our target? Externally: within the client base, who sits on the same cap tables, partnerships, boards, or charities as our prospect? The profiling work above is what surfaces these — the overlaps are the map.
How do we get a referral to a potential client? Make the ask specific, not general. "Do you know anyone who might need wealth advice?" yields nothing; "I'd value an introduction to [named person] — you both sit on [X] — would you feel comfortable making that connection?" yields action. Give the referrer an easy mechanism (a short email you've drafted, a casual introduction at an event) and make it effortless to say yes. Always close the loop and thank them, which earns the next one.
What kind of referral do we want / hope for? Be deliberate about quality over volume. The ideal referral is: a warm, personal introduction (not just a name passed along); to a named, profiled individual who fits the UHNW PE/hedge-fund segment; at or near a liquidity or life event; and routed by someone whose endorsement carries weight with that prospect. A tier of "warm name only" is acceptable as a fallback, but the systematic target is the personally-vouched introduction.
